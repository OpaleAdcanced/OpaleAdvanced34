Opale AdvancedA rich publishing chain for creating pedagogical content
The module

An Opale Advanced module contains :

  • source of knowledge (Learning activity) and/or;
  • self-assessment to identify mastered skills (Self-assessment activity).

These activities can be grouped by steps (division).

The Learning activity

The Learning activity is composed of:

  • Content elements to expose information ;
  • and/or practice activities with contextualization - Question - Solution (Writing task);
  • and/or interactive pedagogical activities (Quizzes) : Single Choice Questions and Multiple Choice Questions, Drag & drop, Fill-in the gaps, etc;
  • And at the end of an activity, comprehensive questions that motivate the learner to rephrase what he learned.

The goal of a learning activity is to provide knowledge to the learner while having him actively engage in the learning process.

The course can also be improved by an appropriate pedagogical markup and the information can be grouped in as many titled parts as you like.

The Self-assessment activity

The Self-assessment activity can contain many different quiz types:

  • single choice questions (textual or graphical),
  • multiple choice questions (textual or graphical),
  • matching and jumble exercises,
  • fill-in the gaps,
  • short answer question.

The goal of the self-assessment activity is to validate knowledge acquisition with a wide range of interactive quizzes.

The references

Opale Advanced allows the creation of various reference items:

  • abbreviation ;
  • glossary ;
  • bibliography ;
  • reference .

These references are items that you write once and can call at many occasions while writing your content.

You can also assemble a common reference base that you reuse in your set of training modules.

The resources

The content in Opale is multimedia. You can insert :

  • text ;
  • Computer code listings;
  • Mathematical formulae: odf or LaTeX;
  • Tables : ods ;
  • Schema : odg ;
  • Images : jpg , gif ou png ;
  • Sounds and audio files: mp3 , opus
  • Videos, animations: mp4 , webm , flv , and Flash ;
  • Office documents: Word , Excel , PowerPoint , odp , odt , pdf , zip .
  • Embedded web sites;
  • Remote web code;
  • Java applets.
